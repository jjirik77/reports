Project Overview

About the Program

This multi-workshop initiative is designed for educators, staff, advisors, and academic and workforce leaders who are ready to embed robust, modern, and pedagogically sound AI into teaching and learning across disciplines and institutions. Building on NCyTE-aligned offerings such as CISM and CISSP with AI, Azure and AWS cloud administration, Security+, CMMC and C2M2, and Cybersecurity and AI Systems, the initiative develops AI-enabled capacity in both technical content (cloud, security, privacy, governance) and day-to-day educational practice (course design, advising, assessment, and student support).

A core aim is to establish a visible presence in every U.S. state and U.S. territory, cultivating local communities of practice where faculty, staff, and industry partners co-develop sharable AI teaching resources, cybersecurity-aligned curricula, and implementation playbooks that can be adapted to regional needs.

Through recurring, stackable workshops, participants join a growing network of practitioners who learn together, test AI tools in authentic educational contexts, and collectively shape responsible, future-ready AI use in education.

Program Goals

  • ✓ Build a distributed, national community of practice in which at least one institutional team per state and territory is actively implementing AI-enhanced teaching, learning, and student support aligned with cybersecurity and IT pathways.
  • ✓ Equip educators with practical, course-ready skills in areas such as Teaching CISM and CISSP Using AI, Azure and AWS Cloud Administration with AI services, Security+ and CISA with AI-augmented labs, and CMMC/C2M2-aligned risk and maturity assessments, so they can immediately modernize their curricula.
  • ✓ Help institutions design and run AI-infused learning experiences—Foundations of AI, Applied AI, and integrated Cybersecurity and AI Systems—that improve student engagement and outcomes while remaining grounded in evidence-based pedagogy and strong academic integrity practices.
  • ✓ Support educators in building sustainable, shareable AI implementation artifacts (templates, rubrics, example prompts, lab guides, and policy language) that lower the barrier for colleagues in their state and region to adopt responsible, effective AI in their own courses and services.
  • ✓ Foster ethical, transparent AI use by training educators and students to document AI’s role in their work, critically evaluate AI-generated outputs, and align AI-supported decisions with institutional, regulatory, and professional standards in cybersecurity, privacy, and compliance.
Curriculum Tracks

Workshop Agenda

Explore our full library of 12 distinct, stackable AI-enhanced workshops tailored for cybersecurity, cloud operations, and compliance educators.

WORKSHOP 01

Teaching CISM Using AI

Prepares faculty to use AI tools to analyze governance and risk case studies, generate draft policies, and help learners practice CISM-aligned decision making with AI-supported feedback loops.

WORKSHOP 02

Azure Cloud Administration

Enables faculty to design labs where participants use Azure-native AI and automation services to deploy, monitor, and secure cloud workloads as part of cloud administration outcomes.

WORKSHOP 03

Security+ for Community College Faculty

Equips faculty to embed AI-assisted threat analysis, log review, and control selection into Security+ labs so learners practice core objectives with AI as a co-analyst rather than a black box.

WORKSHOP 04

CISSP for Community College Faculty

Helps faculty weave AI-supported architecture modeling, risk analysis, and control mapping into CISSP domain coverage so participants learn to critically supervise AI recommendations.

WORKSHOP 05

Cybersecurity and AI Systems

Combines system-focused and AI-focused security by having participants both configure and test AI-driven defenses and analyze how to secure AI models themselves, using AI to generate attack scenarios, detect weaknesses, and design layered mitigations.

WORKSHOP 06

AWS Cloud Administration

Supports faculty in crafting AWS labs that use AI-based monitoring, prediction, and configuration recommendations to deepen faculty understanding of cloud reliability and security tradeoffs.

WORKSHOP 07

CISA Using AI

Trains faculty to have learners use AI to pre-analyze evidence, suggest control gaps, and draft audit narratives while maintaining human oversight for CISA-style governance and assurance work.

WORKSHOP 08

Teaching CISSP Using AI

Enables faculty to structure CISSP courses where learners rely on AI to generate architecture options and policy drafts, then critique and refine them against the official domains and best practices.

WORKSHOP 09

CMMC

Participants use AI tools to map organizational practices to CMMC levels, generate draft SSPs and POA&Ms, and iteratively refine compliance artifacts under instructor guidance while documenting AI’s role for transparency.

WORKSHOP 10

C2M2 (Energy-Sector Focus)

Learners apply AI-assisted maturity assessments to energy-sector scenarios to score capabilities, prioritize improvements, and visualize roadmap options aligned with the Cybersecurity Capability Maturity Model.

WORKSHOP 11

Foundations of AI

Faculty gain a conceptual and hands-on understanding of core AI methods and limitations, using small projects to practice prompting, evaluating outputs, and articulating where human judgment must remain in the loop.

WORKSHOP 12

Applied AI

Participants integrate AI into realistic workflows—such as incident response, compliance, or curriculum design—designing, prototyping, and iterating AI-powered solutions with explicit metrics for effectiveness and risk.
