About the Initiative

This multi-workshop initiative is designed for educators, staff, advisors, and academic and workforce leaders who are ready to embed robust, modern, and pedagogically sound AI into teaching and learning across disciplines and institutions. Building on NCyTE-aligned offerings such as CISM and Ethical Hacking with AI, OT/IT with AI, Azure cloud administration, Security+, CMMC and C2M2, and Cybersecurity and AI Systems, the initiative develops AI-enabled capacity in both technical content (cloud, security, privacy, governance) and day-to-day educational practice (course design, advising, assessment, and student support).

A core aim is to establish a visible presence in every U.S. state and U.S. territory, cultivating local communities of practice where faculty, staff, and industry partners co-develop sharable AI teaching resources, cybersecurity-aligned curricula, and implementation playbooks that can be adapted to regional needs. Through workshops, participants join a growing network of practitioners who learn together, test AI tools in authentic educational contexts, and collectively shape responsible, future-ready AI use in education.

Program Goals

  • Build a distributed, national community of practice in which at least one institutional team per state and territory is actively implementing AI-enhanced teaching, learning, and student support aligned with cybersecurity and IT pathways.
  • Equip educators with practical, course-ready skills in areas such as Teaching CISM, Ethical Hacking, and OT/IT Using AI, Azure Cloud Administration with AI services, Security+ and CISA with AI-augmented labs, and CMMC/C2M2-aligned risk and maturity assessments, so they can immediately modernize their curricula.
  • Help institutions design and run AI-infused learning experiences—Foundations of AI, Applied AI, and integrated Cybersecurity and AI Systems—that improve student engagement and outcomes while remaining grounded in evidence-based pedagogy and strong academic integrity practices.
  • Support educators in building sustainable, shareable AI implementation artifacts (templates, rubrics, example prompts, lab guides, and policy language) that lower the barrier for colleagues in their state and region to adopt responsible, effective AI in their own courses and services.
  • Foster ethical, transparent AI use by training educators and students to document AI’s role in their work, critically evaluate AI-generated outputs, and align AI-supported decisions with institutional, regulatory, and professional standards in cybersecurity, privacy, and compliance.

Key Learning Outcomes

  • Cybersecurity & Computing Context — gain deep functional comprehension of how the workshop topic and related AI capabilities can enhance teaching and learning in their own cybersecurity or computing context.
  • Practical AI-Enabled Workflows — obtain hands-on knowledge of at least one practical AI-enabled workflow, tool, or strategy connected to the workshop focus.
  • Skill-Building Student Activities — guide students to develop skills through an instructional activity, example, or resource that reflects the workshop’s concepts and tools.
  • Curriculum & Standards Alignment — develop and refine an idea, assignment, or lab that aligns the workshop content with relevant standards, frameworks, or credentials.
  • Implementation & Pilot Planning — plan concrete next steps to implement and pilot the new practices with students.

Professional Development

Project Workshops

Professional-development workshops preparing faculty to integrate AI across cybersecurity and IT credentials.

Workshop 01

Teaching CISM Using AI

Prepares faculty to use AI tools to analyze governance and risk case studies, generate draft policies, and help students practice CISM-aligned decision making with AI-supported feedback loops.

Workshop 02

Azure Cloud Administration

Enables faculty to design labs where students use Azure-native AI and automation services to deploy, monitor, and secure cloud workloads as part of cloud administration outcomes.

Workshop 03

Security+ for Community College Faculty

Equips faculty to embed AI-assisted threat analysis, log review, and control selection into Security+ labs so students practice core objectives with AI as a co-analyst rather than a black box.

Workshop 04

Ethical Hacking using AI

Shows participants how to use AI to accelerate ethical hacking workflows—such as reconnaissance, vulnerability discovery, exploit testing, and reporting—while also examining how attackers misuse AI and how defenders can counter AI-driven threats.

Workshop 05

Cybersecurity and AI Systems

Combines system-focused and AI-focused security by having learners both configure and test AI-driven defenses and analyze how to secure AI models themselves, using AI to generate attack scenarios, detect weaknesses, and design layered mitigations.

Workshop 07

CISA Using AI

Trains faculty to have students use AI to pre-analyze evidence, suggest control gaps, and draft audit narratives while maintaining human oversight for CISA-style governance and assurance work.

Workshop 08

Teaching CISSP Using AI

Enables faculty to structure CISSP courses where students rely on AI to generate architecture options and policy drafts, then critique and refine them against the official domains and best practices.

Workshop 09

CMMC

Students use AI tools to map organizational practices to CMMC levels, generate draft SSPs and POA&Ms, and iteratively refine.

Workshop 10

C2M2 Using AI

Guides faculty on how to help students use AI to assess cybersecurity maturity levels, analyze capability maturity profiles across domains, and draft remediation recommendations according to the C2M2 framework.

Workshop 11

Foundations of AI in Cybersecurity

Equips instructors to design and implement introductory course content on AI principles, data governance, and the fundamental technical mechanics of machine learning within IT and security paths.

Workshop 12

Applied AI in IT Systems

Focuses on integrating machine learning toolkits directly into educational practice, enabling educators to build custom AI labs and grading rubrics that simulate real-world industry applications.

Workshop 13

OT/IT with AI

Explores how AI can help secure and monitor converged OT/IT environments by enhancing visibility, anomaly detection, and incident response across industrial control systems and traditional enterprise networks.
