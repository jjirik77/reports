Education Pathway National Center (EPNC)

Interactive Dashboard

The NCAE-C Education Pathway National Center (EPNC) addresses a critical national priority by strengthening the talent pipeline to meet the increasing demands of the federal workforce. By linking high schools directly to National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cybersecurity (NCAE-C), we empower local school systems with flexible, high-quality, and standards-based cybersecurity education.

Our Purpose

To build a sustainable and inclusive pipeline of cybersecurity talent by creating robust pathways from high school programs directly to National Centers of Academic Excellence (NCAE-C).

Our Scope

We deliver an integrated strategy blending open-source curriculum, faculty development, and a national virtual cyber range to empower educators and students nationwide.

Educator Impact

We empower the people who guide students. Our comprehensive professional development has equipped over 3,500 educators with skills in everything from network defense to ethical hacking, delivered through flexible in-person and online formats. We also strategically engage career counselors and advisors, providing them with the toolkits and knowledge to confidently guide students into cybersecurity careers.

Collaboration & Equity

Our impact is amplified through a powerful network of state education agencies, local districts, and NCAE-C institutions. Through hundreds of stakeholder engagements and state summits, we foster cross-sector collaboration to build sustainable pathways. A core focus on equity ensures we are increasing participation in rural and under-resourced communities, making high-tech career pipelines accessible to all students.

Future Vision: Aligning with the DoD 8140/DCWF

Building a DoD-Ready Cyber Workforce: Pathways from K-12 to Federal Service

Our vision is to build a robust, scalable, and DoD-ready cyber talent pipeline. We achieve this by intentionally aligning K-12 cybersecurity education with the DoD Cyber Workforce Framework (DCWF) and creating seamless pathways for students to advance to NCAE-C designated institutions—the primary feeders for the federal workforce.

Promote AI & DCWF Integration

Integrate AI competencies and map all curriculum modules to DCWF work roles, ensuring students are prepared for the "Future Ready" focus of DoD 8140.

Expand Role-Focused Virtual Labs

Broaden access to our national virtual cyber range, with every lab and challenge matched to specific DCWF tasks and skills for hands-on, role-based training.

Recognize High-Performing Pathways

Establish formal recognition for K-12 programs that demonstrate strong, articulated pathways to NCAE-C institutions, accelerating entry into the federal talent pipeline.

Scale Work-Based Learning

Invest in industry onboarding processes to scale internships and apprenticeships, providing the blend of academic and occupational experience recognized by DoD 8140.

Ensure Sustainable Funding

Secure renewed and extended funding to ensure the long-term sustainability of our national pathway model, safeguarding the federal investment in a future-ready workforce.

Bridge the Digital Divide

Launch targeted programs to address infrastructure disparities, ensuring all students, including those in rural and under-resourced areas, can access DCWF-aligned training.

Empower Counselors & Advisors

Enhance professional development for counselors, equipping them to advise students on specific DoD cyber workforce roles and certification pathways.

Champion Gamified Learning

Expand our library of gamified modules and virtual competitions to boost student motivation and retention of DCWF-aligned knowledge and skills.

Foster DCWF-Aligned Curriculum

Continuously evolve our open-source curriculum to be modular, allowing educators to easily adapt content to meet local standards while maintaining DCWF alignment.

Sustain & Adapt to DoD Needs

Establish a continuous improvement cycle to keep our programs responsive to the evolving DoD 8140 directive and the dynamic cybersecurity landscape.
