Securing the Future Through Unified Education

The EPNC bridges the gap between high school classrooms and National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cybersecurity. We are building the national workforce pipeline, state by state.

Our Purpose

To create seamless transitions for high school students into NCAE-C designated programs, ensuring a consistent, high-quality cybersecurity education that meets federal standards.

Our Scope

Managing a nationwide ecosystem of curriculum development, educator training, and a cloud-based virtual range accessible to every underserved school district.

Sector Growth Highlights

Lessons Learned

  • • Modular curriculum works best for regional adaptations.
  • • Gamified labs significantly boost retention.
  • • Academic advisors are the primary gatekeepers.

Key Challenges

  • • High-latency internet in rural districts.
  • • Recruiting technical faculty in low-income areas.
  • • Aligning diverse state standards with NCAE-C.

Recommendations

  • • Renew and extend multi-year grant funding.
  • • Establish a formal school recognition badge.
  • • Invest in automated lab onboarding tools.

Strategic Trajectory (FY25-27)

Advancing beyond foundations into AI-driven defense and expanded work-based placements.

AI Integration

Embed Generative AI and ML defense modules into high school pathways.

Workforce Readiness

Formal apprenticeship pipeline for students reaching Level 4 completion.

Infrastructure Parity

Direct hardware grants for low-connectivity school districts.

Standardization

Achieve DoD 8140/DCWF alignment for all entry-level curriculum.
