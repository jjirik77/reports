Executive Summary

Cyber-Illinois.org represents a transformative regional initiative designed to cultivate a robust cybersecurity ecosystem throughout Illinois. Led by local business leaders and the distinguished Center for Systems Security and Information Assurance (CSSIA) staff at Moraine Valley Community College, this project unites the Chicago area business community with Illinois' Centers of Academic Excellence in Cyber Defense (CAE-CD) designated institutions.

The initiative addresses a critical need: developing diverse cybersecurity talent pipelines that span from K-12 through higher education. By engaging regional school districts and establishing sustainable cybersecurity programs of study, the project creates articulation agreements connecting K-12 students with community colleges and universities. This comprehensive approach ensures that cybersecurity career pathways are promoted early and sustained throughout students' educational journeys.

The primary objectives focus on developing and enhancing cybersecurity education programs while creating clear career pathways responsive to Illinois' dynamic workforce needs. This community-driven network analyzes regional cybersecurity workforce demands, ensuring educational programs align with industry standards and integrate seamlessly across educational levels. By bridging academia, industry, and government, Cyber Illinois establishes a sustainable model for addressing the state's cybersecurity workforce challenges.

Project Goals and Objectives

The Cyber Illinois RAMPS initiative operates according to five interconnected goals that comprehensively address cybersecurity workforce development needs. Each goal includes specific objectives and measurable metrics ensuring accountability and continuous improvement.

Goal 1: Promote the Discovery of Cybersecurity Careers and Multiple Pathways

  • Objective 1a: Establish a local advisory team to collect and analyze cybersecurity-related workforce needs.
    Metric: Document membership, attendance, and contributions to RAMPS community data collection efforts.
  • Objective 1b: Identify representatives from major economic sectors including healthcare, manufacturing, energy, transportation, finance, retail, education, and government.
    Metric: Analyze RAMPS community representation across each industry sector.

Goal 2: Transform Learning to Build and Sustain a Diverse and Skilled Workforce

  • Objective 2a: Host two events annually to review current academic cybersecurity curriculum, instructional materials, and programs of study.
    Metric: Document attendance and activities at each event.
  • Objective 2b: Develop instruments to collect and provide feedback to academic institutions regarding workforce needs and diversity expansion.
    Metric: Review data collection instruments for accuracy, relevance, and impact.
  • Objective 2c: Analyze community feedback for updating and expanding local academic programs.
    Metric: Generate a report summarizing the use of community feedback in updating and expanding cybersecurity academic programs.

Goal 3: Modernize the Talent Management Process to Address Cybersecurity Skills Gaps

  • Objective 3a: Utilize cybersecurity workforce summary reports to publish talent gap analysis.
    Metric: Document summary report results.
  • Objective 3b: Analyze cybersecurity talent gap results to identify industry credentials, workforce competencies, and required knowledge and skills.
    Metric: Publish cybersecurity talent gap analysis results.
  • Objective 3c: Use cybersecurity talent gap analysis to make academic updates and program expansion recommendations.
    Metric: Document changes and impact of cybersecurity academic programs based on talent gap analysis results.

Goal 4: Expand Use of the Workforce Framework for Cybersecurity (NICE Framework)

  • Objective 4a: Perform an applicability study comparing the local workforce to the Workforce Framework for Cybersecurity.
    Metric: Document alignment and potential gaps between local workforce applicability study and the NICE Framework.
  • Objective 4b: Leverage the NCAE-C cybersecurity workforce competencies database.
    Metric: Document proper utilization of the NCAE-C cybersecurity workforce competencies database.
  • Objective 4c: Partner with local academic institutions and employers to develop and promote cybersecurity credentials aligned with workforce needs.
    Metric: Document academic credentials and industry certifications targeted by stakeholders.

Goal 5: Drive Research on Effective Practices for Cybersecurity Workforce Development

  • Objective 5a: Develop and deliver cybersecurity awareness campaigns to strengthen Illinois' cybersecurity posture.
    Metric: Document the number of participants completing awareness training.
  • Objective 5b: Partner with academia to develop and implement new credentials meeting local workforce needs.
    Metric: Document the number of new academic lessons, courses, certifications, and degrees.
  • Objective 5c: Partner with local stakeholders to promote industry certifications aligned with local workforce needs.
    Metric: Document the number of participants and employees earning industry certifications.

Year One Summary

The Cyber Illinois RAMPS initiative successfully completed its foundational first year by establishing a robust, collaborative ecosystem addressing the regional cybersecurity workforce pipeline. The Center planned and implemented four strategic events that brought together academic institutions, industry leaders, government partners, and community stakeholders.

Inaugural RAMPS Committee Summit (March 21, 2025)

The project launched with a comprehensive summit that united Midwest NCAE-CD institutions alongside key industry leaders, as well as government partners. This pivotal event formally launched the IllinoisCyber.org resource hub and established a formal advisory team that set the strategic roadmap for the project. Participants engaged in professional development focused on emerging technologies and initiated critical alignment between education programs and industry-recognized credentials.

Career Counselors and Academic Advisors Training

The RAMPS team developed and delivered specialized training designed for key stakeholders who guide students into cybersecurity careers. The comprehensive toolkit and activities educated counselors and advisors about the cybersecurity workforce landscape and available academic programs throughout the region, enabling them to provide informed guidance to students exploring cybersecurity pathways.

Cybersecurity Awareness Month Event (October 3, 2025)

During National Cybersecurity Awareness Month, the team hosted a significant engagement event drawing over 70 students and 20 K-12 teachers from across the region. Participants engaged in an interactive "cybersecurity sticker heist" activity that demonstrated security concepts through hands-on learning. The event also attracted eight business leaders, government officials, and local leaders, and featured an AI workshop addressing emerging technologies' impact on cybersecurity. This multi-generational gathering successfully bridged K-12, higher education, and industry communities.

Women in Technology Dinner

This established college event expanded to incorporate RAMPS objectives by connecting women working in cybersecurity with industry speakers. The dinner fostered networking opportunities and mentorship connections while promoting diversity and inclusion in the cybersecurity workforce, directly supporting the initiative's commitment to building a diverse talent pipeline.

Special Highlights: Strategic Partnership (NCYTE)

The Illinois RAMPS project established a strategic partnership with the National Cybersecurity Training & Education Center (NCYTE) that significantly amplifies project impact while ensuring long-term sustainability. This collaboration leverages NCYTE's established infrastructure and national NSF funding (Grant #2054724) to deliver highly relevant online training supporting the RAMPS grant's core goal of transforming learning to build a diverse and skilled workforce.

Industry Certification and Core Competency Training

The partnership provides faculty with essential training in major industry-recognized credentials, ensuring academic curricula remain aligned with current workforce demands. Multi-day workshops focus on three of the most sought-after certifications:

  • (ISC)Â² Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP): This foundational training enables faculty to teach students how to design, implement, and manage best-in-class cybersecurity programs. The comprehensive curriculum covers all eight CISSP domains, preparing students for this globally recognized certification.
  • ISACA Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA) and Certified Information Security Manager (CISM): These specialized workshops provide training in audit, control, security, and governance, specifically targeting professionals transitioning from technical roles to management positions. Faculty trained in these credentials can develop courses preparing students for senior cybersecurity roles. This focus on core, high-level credentials ensures that graduates from Illinois CAE-CD institutions enter the workforce immediately competitive for advanced positions, addressing the state's need for experienced cybersecurity professionals.

Practical and Offensive Security Skills

To ensure faculty can teach practical, hands-on skills relevant to modern threats, the NCYTE collaboration included workshops focused on offensive security and emerging technologies:

  • EC-Council Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH): This immersive workshop places participants in the hacker mindset, covering topics like SQL injection, spear phishing, and buffer overflows through live demonstrations and hands-on lab exercises. Faculty completing this training can develop practical courses teaching students to think like attackers, a critical skill for modern cybersecurity defenders.
  • Automotive Security and Car Hacking Workshop: This specialized training addressed the exponentially increasing attack surface of modern, autonomous vehicles. Available to faculty from various departments including Automotive Technology and Computer Science, the workshop equipped educators to incorporate content on V2X technologies and vehicle control systems into existing courses. This cross-disciplinary approach recognizes that cybersecurity increasingly intersects with traditional technical fields.

Addressing the Impact of Artificial Intelligence

Recognizing that Artificial Intelligence represents both a powerful tool and a critical threat vector, the partnership scheduled two distinct workshops dedicated to this emerging field, aligning perfectly with the RAMPS project's emphasis on evolving threats.

  • Implications of Artificial Intelligence for Educators: This workshop, highlighted as a major Year One accomplishment, specifically targeted university, community college, and high school faculty, staff, counselors, and academic advisors. The training focused on practical integration of AI for personalized student learning, curriculum design, adaptive assessments, and teaching ethical AI use in the classroom. By preparing educators to leverage AI effectively, the project ensures that Illinois students graduate with essential AI literacy alongside cybersecurity expertise.
  • AI Threat Management Workshop: Complementing the faculty-focused AI training, this workshop equipped cybersecurity professionals with advanced skills to combat AI-driven threats. Participants engaged in hands-on lab exercises learning to use data science and AI methodologies for proactive threat detection, vulnerability assessment, penetration testing, and intelligence gathering. Crucially, content extended beyond technical skills to cover AI ethics, governance, risk management, and compliance, ensuring attendees developed holistic expertise needed to build secure AI environments and conduct Privacy Impact Assessments.

The Role of NCYTE in Project Sustainability

The NCYTE partnership is vital to the Illinois RAMPS project's long-term sustainability model. By leveraging NCYTE's existing National Science Foundation grant funding and curriculum development expertise, the RAMPS project minimizes direct cost burdens while maximizing training quality and reach. This approach institutionalizes project impact by connecting the Illinois community directly to a nationally recognized pipeline for faculty development.

This strategic resource sharing allows the Cyber Illinois team to focus RAMPS grant funds on regional stakeholder engagement, workforce analysis, and implementing specific academic program changes based on collected data. Rather than developing training materials from scratch, the project accesses proven, high-quality content that has already undergone rigorous development and validation.
